I ignored her, and spent a year typing numbers until they matched the answers in the back of the book. I understood nothing.

Working with AI is one continuous loop

01
Discuss & Design
Talk through the next move; AI proposes it — then argues each design decision and models the physics.
↳ skills carry my design rules in, so it reasons from my constraints
02
Build & Iterate
Custom tools and artifacts get built along the way — often to make the decisions, not just to carry them out.
03
Test
The design meets the real thing, and the real thing decides. Reality is the pressure test.
04
Analyze Data
AI processes the results and runs the stats.
↳ I give the direction — it writes the custom Python. It does not run itself.
DOCUMENT Captured continuously — not a step at the end. Because the discussion happens in text, the results arrive already analysed and presentation-ready. The documentation is written while the test is running.

Nothing here ships and stops — the real thing answers back, and the loop comes round again.
